1. **Cafes & restaurants** — live and die by Google stars
2. **Hair salons & barbers** — reputation-driven, repeat customers
3. **Dental & physio clinics** — high-value customers, reviews matter hugely
4. **Gyms & fitness studios** — competitive, need differentiation
5. **Auto mechanics** — trust-based, reviews are everything

### Skip these:
- Chain stores (can't make decisions)
- Businesses with 4.8+ stars and 500+ reviews (they don't need you)
- Places that look dead/closing

## The Approach

### Step 0: Research (30 seconds outside)
Pull up their Google listing on your phone before you walk in. Note:
- Their star rating
- How many reviews they have
- Their most recent negative review (if visible)
- A nearby competitor with better stars

### Step 1: Walk in and get to the decision maker
**Don't pitch the first person you see.** Ask:

> "Hey, is the owner or manager around? I've got something quick to show them about their Google reviews."

If they're not there:
> "No worries — can I leave this for them?" (hand them the leave-behind) "It's about getting more 5-star reviews on Google. My number's on there."

If they ARE there, move to Step 2.

### Step 2: The Opening (15 seconds)

**Don't say "I'm selling software." Lead with their problem.**

> "Hey, I just looked up your Google page — you've got [X] stars from [Y] reviews. The thing is, your happy customers probably never think to leave a review. It's always the unhappy ones who bother. I built something that fixes that — takes 2 minutes to show you."

**If they say they're busy:**
> "Totally get it. Can I leave this? It explains everything." (hand leave-behind) "Or I can come back — when's a quieter time?"

**If they're interested, move to Step 3.**

### Step 3: The Demo (2 minutes)

**Show, don't tell. Use your phone the entire time.**

1. **Show the QR code card:**
   > "This goes on your counter. Customer scans it with their phone camera."

2. **Scan the QR code yourself and show the review form:**
   > "They see this — tap the stars, leave a comment. Takes 20 seconds."

3. **Explain the smart routing:**
   > "Here's the magic — if they give you 4 or 5 stars, they get nudged to leave that review on Google. If they give you 1, 2, or 3 stars, it stays private — you get a notification so you can fix it before it goes public."

4. **Show the dashboard (on your phone):**
   > "You get a dashboard showing all your reviews, who's happy, who's not, and your response rate."

### Step 4: The Close

> "It's $79 a month, but the first 2 weeks are free. I can set you up right now — literally takes 2 minutes. Want to try it?"

**If yes:**
- Sign them up on your phone right there
- Place the QR code card on their counter
- Show them the dashboard
- Get their phone number for follow-up

**If "I need to think about it":**
> "No worries at all. I'll leave this card here — it's got the QR code already working. If any customers scan it, you'll see the reviews come in. Here's my number if you want to get set up properly."

Leave the QR code card AND the leave-behind. Follow up in 2 days.

**If "we already use something":**
> "Oh yeah? Who are you with?" (They'll say Podium, Birdeye, etc.)
> "How much do you pay?" (They'll say $200-400/mo)
> "I do the same thing for $79. Happy to show you side-by-side if you're ever up for a switch."

**If hard no:**
> "No worries, thanks for your time!" (leave immediately, don't be pushy)

## After the Visit

### If they signed up:
- Send them a text: "Hey [name], Tristan here from InsightReviews. You're all set up! Your review link is [URL]. Let me know if you need anything."
- Check on them after 3 days — "How's it going? Getting any scans?"
- Check again at day 10 — "Your trial ends in 4 days — want to keep going?"

### If they took a leave-behind:
- Note the business name and address
- Follow up in 2 days — walk back in or call
- "Hey, I left some info about InsightReviews the other day. Did [owner] get a chance to look at it?"

### If they took a QR card:
- Check your dashboard — if scans come in from their area, they're using it
- Follow up in 3 days whether or not you see scans

## Objection Handling

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|
| "We don't need more reviews" | "How many Google reviews do you have? [competitor] down the road has [more]. Customers compare." |
| "We already ask customers to review us" | "How many actually do it? With this, they scan a QR code and it takes 20 seconds. Way easier than 'please go to Google and search for us and click review.'" |
| "$79 is too much" | "One new customer from a Google review is worth way more than $79. And you can cancel anytime." |
| "We tried something like this before" | "What didn't work? This is dead simple — QR code on the counter, customer scans, done. No app to download, no account to create." |
| "Can I try it free?" | "Yep, 14 days free. I'll set you up right now." |
| "Send me an email" | "Sure, but let me just show you the QR code real quick — 30 seconds." (Then demo it. Email follow-ups get ignored.) |
