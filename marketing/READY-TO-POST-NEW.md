## Post 1: r/sweatystartup

**Title:** The simple system that took my mate's plumbing business from 12 to 130+ Google reviews in 6 months

**Body:**

Had a beer with a mate last weekend who runs a plumbing business (6 trucks, mostly residential). A year ago he was sitting on about 12 Google reviews and losing jobs to competitors who had 80+. He reckoned he was doing better work but just never asked for reviews.

Here's what he changed — and honestly it's dead simple.

**The core problem:** You finish a job, the customer's happy, you drive off... and nobody ever leaves a review. By the time you send a follow-up email 2 days later, they've moved on. Email open rates are about 20% anyway so most people never even see it.

**What actually works: SMS within the hour.**

SMS open rates are north of 95%. That's not a typo. Almost everyone reads a text message.

His system now:

1. Job gets marked complete in their scheduling software
2. Admin sends a text within the hour while the customer still has that "fixed problem" high
3. Text is short and personal: "Hey [name], thanks for choosing [business]. If you had a good experience, we'd really appreciate a quick Google review — here's the link: [link]"
4. That's literally it

**Why it works so well for trades:**

- Timing. The customer just watched you fix something that was stressing them out. They're grateful RIGHT NOW
- SMS cuts through. Nobody ignores a text the way they ignore emails
- The direct Google link removes friction. No searching, no figuring out where to leave it. One tap
- Trades are personal. You were in their house. There's a human connection that makes people want to help

**The numbers back it up.** Harvard research found that even a half-star increase on your rating can drive 5-9% more revenue. For a plumbing business doing $1.2M, that's potentially $60-100K in extra work just from better reviews. Mental.

**Extra tips that helped him:**

- He printed a small sign for the vans: "Love our work? Leave us a Google review" with a QR code. Low effort, picks up a few extras
- He responds to EVERY review, good or bad, within 24 hours. Google seems to reward businesses that engage
- For negative feedback, he calls the customer directly before they go public. Most of the time he can sort it out and they either update or remove the review
- He asks his best customers specifically. "Mate, would you mind mentioning the [specific thing] in your review?" Detailed reviews rank better

**The mindset shift** is just making it part of your process. Same as sending an invoice or doing a site clean-up. It's not begging — you did good work and you're making it easy for them to say so.

He went from 12 reviews (4.1 stars) to 130+ (4.8 stars) and reckons the phone rings noticeably more now. New customers literally tell him "you had the best reviews."

If you're a tradie or service business sitting on single-digit reviews, honestly just start texting customers after every job. You'll be shocked how many people are happy to leave one — they just needed the nudge and the link.

Happy to answer any questions if anyone's working on this for their business.

## Post 2: r/restaurateur

**Title:** We went from 3.9 to 4.6 stars on Google in 4 months. Here's the unsexy truth about how.

**Body:**

I help run a family cafe in Melbourne (60 seats, breakfast and lunch). At the start of last year we were sitting on a 3.9 Google rating with about 85 reviews. The frustrating thing was, we KNEW most customers loved us — regulars, strong word of mouth, good trade. But our online rating didn't reflect it.

The problem most hospo owners have is that unhappy customers leave reviews unprompted. Happy customers just... come back. So your rating skews negative over time unless you actively do something about it.

Here's what actually moved the needle for us.

**Step 1: Accept that you need volume.**

You can't fight 5 bad reviews with good vibes. You need 50 good reviews to bury them. The maths is just how averages work. So the goal becomes: make it stupidly easy for happy customers to leave reviews.

**Step 2: SMS beats everything else.**

We tried the "leave us a review!" table tent thing. We tried QR codes on receipts. We tried emailing our mailing list. All of these barely moved the needle.

What actually worked: collecting the customer's mobile number (we already did this for waitlist/reservations) and texting them a review link a couple of hours after their meal.

The stats tell you why — SMS has a 95%+ open rate compared to about 20% for email. When someone gets a text, they read it. When they get an email from a restaurant, it goes straight to promotions or trash.

**Step 3: Timing matters heaps.**

We found 2-3 hours after the meal is the sweet spot. They've had a good experience, they're home on the couch, they get a quick text: "Hey [name], thanks for coming in today! If you enjoyed it, a quick Google review would mean the world to us: [link]"

Not next day. Not next week. Same day, while they still remember what they ate.

**Step 4: Make the link go directly to the review box.**

This is the one most people stuff up. Don't just link to your Google Business profile. Use the direct review link that opens the star rating straight away. If someone has to click around to figure out where to leave a review, you've lost them. Google has a specific URL format for this — search "Google review link generator" and you'll find it.

**Step 5: Handle negative feedback before it goes public.**

This one's big. If someone's had a bad experience, you ideally want to hear about it directly rather than reading it on Google. We started asking for feedback via the same SMS process, and if someone indicated they weren't happy, we'd call them personally to sort it out. Most of the time, they just wanted to be heard. Way better than a 1-star review sitting there forever.

88% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations. That's the reality we're operating in now. Your Google rating IS your reputation.

**The results:**

- Went from 85 reviews (3.9 stars) to 300+ reviews (4.6 stars) in about 4 months
- Weekday trade picked up noticeably — new customers finding us through Google Maps
- We still get the occasional stinker but it barely dents the average now because of the volume

We ended up using a tool called InsightReviews (insightreviews.com.au) to automate the SMS side of things — staff just punch in the number at the till and it handles the rest. But honestly, even doing it manually from your phone would work if you're disciplined about it. The key is just doing it consistently.

Happy to go into more detail on any of this. It's not rocket science but it genuinely changed our business.

## Post 3: r/AutoDetailing

**Title:** You're doing $500 corrections and getting zero reviews for it. Here's how to fix that.

**Body:**

Something I've noticed in detailing is that we do incredibly visual, satisfying work — the kind of stuff that makes people go "holy shit" when they see their car — and then we just... hand them the keys and move on.

Meanwhile the local car wash with 400 Google reviews is outranking you in Maps even though their idea of a detail is a chamois and some tyre shine.

I've been refining how I collect reviews over the past year and I reckon the single biggest unlock for detailers specifically is **asking at the reveal moment.**

Think about it. There is no better time in any service business to ask for a review than the exact second a customer sees their paint correction for the first time. They're genuinely amazed. Their car looks better than when they bought it. They're taking photos. That emotional peak is your window.

Here's the process I've landed on:

**1. The walkthrough IS the ask.**

When you do the final walkthrough with the customer, take them around the car, show them the panels you corrected, let them feel the coating. While they're buzzing, say something like: "Glad you're happy with it. Hey, if you get a minute, a Google review would really help me out — I'll text you the link so it's easy."

That's it. No awkward card. No QR code they'll never scan. Just a genuine ask while they're stoked.

**2. Text them the link within 30 minutes.**

As soon as they drive off, send a text: "Thanks for trusting me with the [car model], [name]! If you're happy with the result, a quick review here would mean heaps: [direct Google review link]"

Why text and not email? 95%+ of texts get read. About 20% of emails do. Most of your customers will see the text while they're still admiring their car in the driveway.

**3. Ask for specifics.**

If you've got a good rapport with the customer (which you usually do after spending 6 hours on their car), ask them to mention what you did. "Would you mind mentioning the paint correction?" or "If you could mention the ceramic coating that'd be awesome."

Reviews that mention specific services help you rank for those search terms. "Best paint correction in [city]" is worth its weight in gold.

**4. The before/after play.**

This one's detailing-specific and powerful. Text them their before/after photos along with the review link. Two reasons:
- It reminds them just how dramatic the transformation was
- They'll often share those photos in the review itself, which makes the review more compelling for future customers reading it

**5. Catch the unhappy ones early.**

Once in a while someone's not 100% satisfied — maybe they expected perfection on a car with deep scratches, whatever. You want to hear about that BEFORE they put it on Google. If your follow-up process gives them a way to tell you directly, you can usually sort it out. Maybe touch up one panel, explain what was realistic. Most reasonable people appreciate the effort and don't leave a bad review.

**The business case:**

Research shows a half-star improvement can mean 5-9% more revenue. If you're doing $150K a year, that's $7-15K extra just from having a better rating. And in detailing, where the average job value is high and customers are choosing between 3-4 options on Google Maps, reviews are basically everything.

I've been using a tool called InsightReviews to handle the texting automatically — my admin just enters the customer's number when they pick up and it sends the review request. Saves me remembering to do it manually after every job. But even if you just set a reminder on your phone to text every customer, you'll see results.

The main thing is: stop leaving reviews to chance. You're already doing amazing work. Just make it easy for people to say so.

Keen to hear if anyone else has cracked the review game in detailing — always looking to improve.
